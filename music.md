# music

Music has long been a huge part of my life; I have been playing violin since I was 6 and formally started composition lessons in 2019, though I've been improvising casually at the piano for far longer. Since then, I've been a part of countless orchestras and ensembles, written lots of music for various occasions, and met a lot of great people. At Brown, I was a violinist in the Brown University Orchestra and Chamber Music Program, as well as a member of the Fermata Composers Collective.

On the academic side, I've explored theory, composition, and musicology, and have enjoyed every class I've taken. I've also worked as a teaching assistant for the introductory music theory course this past fall. I recently worked as an undergraduate researcher with the PALM lab at Brown, where I explored the internal representations of music theory in music generative models. My senior year honors thesis project in music was an orchestration of 3 of Rachmaninoff's Études-Tableaux (op. 33 d minor, op.39 no.5, and op.39 no.8, for those who memorize Rachmaninoff opus numbers), and it was incredibly rewarding to work one-on-one with some of the leading voices in contemporary classical composition.
